User guide

Cheat Sheet

A quick overview of the main actions, mouse controls, menus, and video workflows available in Floorball Shot Plotter.

Back to download page

Use the app for

  • Opponent shots and goals against your team
  • Video-assisted plotting and calibration
  • Video links and shot-specific segments
  • Pass-origin logging
  • Heatmaps and danger-zone overlays
  • Shot Log filtering, save/load, and CSV export

Mouse controls

Rink map / plotting view

  • Left-click on the rink — add an opponent shot at that location.
  • Right-click on the rink — add an opponent goal at that location.
  • Left-click during rink pass-origin selection — mark where the opponent pass came from.

Shot Log

  • Right-click a Shot Log row — open the video-link menu for that event.
  • Link Offline Video — attach a local video file.
  • Link Online Video — attach a video URL.
  • Play / Edit Segment — open a linked local clip and save start/stop times.

Video controls

  • Video → Open Video Clip... — open a video without selecting a shot first.
  • Plot: OFF / ON — turn video plotting mode on or off.
  • Right-click in video while Plot mode is ON — plot at that video location.
  • Right-click during video pass-origin selection — mark the pass origin from video.
  • Left-click during calibration — select the requested rink landmark in the video.
  • Skip Point — skip a calibration landmark that is not visible.

Basic plotting

Add a shot

  1. Left-click the opponent shot location on the rink.
  2. Select game state, opponent context, situation, shot type, shooter hand, and passer details.
  3. The shot appears on the rink and in the Shot Log.

Add a goal

  1. Right-click the opponent goal location on the rink.
  2. Fill in the opponent event details.
  3. The goal appears on the rink and in the Shot Log.

Opponent event dialog

The dialog is written from your team’s defensive perspective. Use it for opponent events only.

  • 5v5 / Even strength — normal opponent attacks.
  • Special teams — power play, shorthanded chances, 6v5, empty net.
  • Restart / set play against — free hit, faceoff, penalty shot against.
  • Broken play / rebound against — rebounds, screens, scrambles, failed clearances.

Pass-origin logging

  1. Choose the opponent passer hand.
  2. When asked, choose to mark the pass origin manually.
  3. On the rink map, left-click the pass-origin location.
  4. In video Plot mode, right-click the pass-origin location in the video.

Use No assist when the shot is unassisted.

Shot Log

The Shot Log table shows result, situation, shot type, hands, period, X/Y, pass-origin X/Y, distance, angle, zone, and video data.

  • Use dropdown filters to focus on specific values.
  • Show/hide columns with the column controls.
  • Collapse the Shot Log to the right with the flap.
  • Drag the border left/right to resize the panel.

Video links for logged shots

  1. Find the shot/goal in the Shot Log.
  2. Right-click that Shot Log row.
  3. Choose Link Offline Video for a local file, or Link Online Video for a URL.
  4. Right-click the same row again and choose Play / Edit Segment to open the clip.
  5. Save start/stop times if only one part of the clip should be linked.
  6. Use Relink or Remove Video Link if you need to change it later.

Plot directly from video

  1. Use Video → Open Video Clip....
  2. Click Plot: OFF so it becomes Plot: ON.
  3. Right-click the shot location in the video.
  4. Choose Add Shot here or Add Goal here.
  5. If needed, right-click the pass-origin location in the video.

Important: video plotting uses right-click. Left-click is used for calibration landmarks.

Video calibration

  1. Open a video.
  2. Click Calibrate.
  3. Left-click each requested rink landmark in the video.
  4. If a landmark is outside the frame, click Skip Point.
  5. When complete, calibration is saved for that video file.

When the same video is reopened, saved calibration should restore automatically.

Heatmaps and overlay

  • Switch between plot, standard heatmap, goal heatmap, and save heatmap views.
  • Use Heatmap → Heatmap Settings... to adjust heatmap behavior.
  • Use Shot Zone → Show Shot Zone Overlay to toggle the semi-transparent danger-zone image.

Calibration landmarks

The app asks for these landmarks when visible:

  1. Lower Left Goal Area
  2. Lower Right Goal Area
  3. Upper Right Goal Area
  4. Upper Left Goal Area
  5. Lower Left Goalie Area
  6. Lower Right Goalie Area
  7. Upper Left Goalie Area
  8. Upper Right Goalie Area
  9. Center of Goal Line
  10. Upper Left Defensive Zone
  11. Upper Right Defensive Zone
  12. Center Faceoff Dot
  13. Center of Defensive Zone
  14. Left Center Defensive Zone
  15. Right Center Defensive Zone
  16. Lower Left Faceoff Dot
  17. Lower Right Faceoff Dot
  18. Behind Goal Near Board

The most reliable points are usually the goal-area and goalie-area corners, goal-line centre, visible faceoff dots, and clear defensive-zone board/line references.

Save, load, export

  • File → Save CSV exports the current log data.
  • File → Load CSV loads previous match/event data.
  • File → Export Image exports the current rink/plot view.

Beta testing checklist

  1. Rink left-click Add Shot works.
  2. Rink right-click Add Goal works.
  3. Shot Log right-click video linking works.
  4. Play / Edit Segment works for linked clips.
  5. Video Plot mode right-click Add Shot/Add Goal works.
  6. Video pass-origin right-click works.
  7. Video calibration left-click landmarks work.
  8. Shot Log filtering, heatmaps, overlays, save/load, and export work.
